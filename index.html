ABOUT

Removing barriers to academic research

Our mission is simple: clear the way between academic findings and those who want to engage with them, so that reading papers becomes an easy and enjoyable experience.

Curated selection

Biblio taps into the brain and hard work of the specialists in the field to bring you the latest insights and unique perspectives on any subject that interests you. Enjoy a perfectly taylored selection of papers everyday, based on your interests and what matters to you.

Customizable reading experience

Not only does Biblio translates jargon-heavy academic language to everyday English, but page layouts are also designed for optimal readability. Make them your own by adjusting font type, text size, background colors and much more.

Fully accessible

We believe in open knowledege for all, and accessibility is our priority number one. Biblio is absolutely free, easy to use, available offline and offers the option to listen to rather than read the paper.

Engaged community

Join open and mind-opening discussions with researchers and people moving thinking forward. Biblio offers you the space to further the conversation, connect direcly with the experts, ask questions and expand your knowledge.

PHILOSOPHY

Giving volume to world-changing knowledge

Academic research is about gathering new information and furthering human knowledge. It has the potential to spur critical thinking, inject nuance and depth to the the public discussion, inform decision-making, empower citizens, come up with new datas and smart solutions and help understand the complex world we live in. However, knowledge produced in universities largely goes unheeded and rarely influences thinking beyond the system in which it was constructed.

To bridge the devide between scholar communities and everyone else, we need to increase the discoverability, comprehension, use and diffusion of academic findings. It’s about time for a global movement that pushes academic evidence out into the world in an accessible format so that it does not sit idle behind journal paywalls but makes a difference in the world in which we live.

FAQ

Answering your questions


Contact Us
  • What's the problem with academic papers?
  • An average paper in a peer-reviewed academic journal is read by no more than 10 people, according to Singapore-based academic Asit Biswas and Oxford researcher Julian Kirchherr. As many as 1.5 million peer-reviewed articles are published every year, but 82percents of them never get cited once, not even by other academics.
  • Will academic writing become obsolete ?
  • No. Biblio is not a refutation of journal publication or the important evidence it imparts, but an extension of the publication process. We can ‘disseminate’ research accessibly while still writing a paper for an academic audience.
  • How does Biblio benefits the researchers ?
  • The biggest hurdle is that academia has yet to find an incentive for researchers to take time away from the lab to engage the public. Universities still operate under an ancient system that values only scholarly output. Establishing a system that also rewards academics who engage and share their knowledge with the public would be a critical step for academics to tell audiences why their research matters.
    In the meantime, Biblio gives academic researchers the opportunity to take back the role they're supposed to play: educating society as a whole. Gratification can also come from communicating concepts in a more open and accessible way and as a result seeing your work active in the world. It also allows researcher to control their voice and message and still push their expertise out to wider audience.
  • How does Biblio work with the researchers ?
  • We are aware that academic researchers cannot be all things to all people. Even if they want to engage with a wider public, they might not know how, and few have the time or the ressources to do it properly. Which is why Biblio acts as a mediator and editorial service. Biblio provides the researchers with guidelines to write a first draft of the digested version of their paper - the dos and don'ts. Our team take it from there, review the original paper and the draft, tightening it up with the needs of our readers in mind. Finally, after a few rounds of edit, we check with the researcher and upon approval, publish the final version on Biblio app.
