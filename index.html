PHILOSOPHY

Giving volume to world-changing knowledge

Academic research is about gathering new information and furthering human knowledge. It has the potential to spur critical thinking, inject nuance and depth to the the public discussion, inform decision-making, empower citizens, come up with new datas and smart solutions and help understand the complex world we live in. However, knowledge produced in universities largely goes unheeded and rarely influences thinking beyond the system in which it was constructed.

To bridge the devide between scholar communities and everyone else, we need to increase the discoverability, comprehension, use and diffusion of academic findings. It’s about time for a global movement that pushes academic evidence out into the world in an accessible format so that it does not sit idle behind journal paywalls but makes a difference in the world in which we live.

What started out as an experiment in 2011 has blossomed into a full media service for academics and a clearing house of high-quality, EvidenceNetwork.ca acts as a mediator and editorial service for academics They are asked to write a rough draft of their op-ed. Our editorial team works on tightening up the op-ed, with the needs of specific media outlets in mind. Rough drafts are often trimmed to specific word counts, the argument is tightened and jargon-laced language is excised in favour of plain or conversational language suiting traditional op-ed style. then check with them The author is part of this process throughout. The op-ed routinely goes through three or more rounds of edits, and if the material is controversial or political in any way, it goes through an informal peer review process with other experts vetting the piece for balance and accuracy. All points of evidence are hyperlinked to their sources. Once we are certain the piece is ready for the media, we secure author approval, and then EvidenceNetwork.ca shops the op-ed to the major media outlets on behalf of the author.
